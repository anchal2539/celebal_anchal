Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the field of computer science focused on building systems
that can perform tasks which normally require human intelligence. These tasks include
learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and language understanding.

History of AI
The term "Artificial Intelligence" was coined in 1956 at the Dartmouth Conference, where
researchers first gathered to discuss the possibility of machines that could think. Early
AI research focused on symbolic reasoning and rule-based systems. In the 1980s, expert
systems became popular, using large sets of hand-crafted rules to mimic human decision
making in narrow domains.

Machine Learning
Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of AI that allows systems to learn patterns from data
rather than being explicitly programmed with rules. Instead of hand-coding logic, ML
algorithms are trained on examples and improve their performance as more data becomes
available. Common types of machine learning include supervised learning, unsupervised
learning, and reinforcement learning.

Deep Learning
Deep Learning is a further subset of machine learning based on artificial neural networks
with many layers. These networks are capable of automatically learning hierarchical
representations of data, which has led to major breakthroughs in image recognition,
speech recognition, and natural language processing. Deep learning became especially
prominent after 2012, when neural networks began outperforming traditional methods on
large benchmark datasets.

Natural Language Processing
Natural Language Processing (NLP) is the branch of AI concerned with enabling computers
to understand, interpret, and generate human language. NLP powers applications such as
machine translation, chatbots, sentiment analysis, and search engines. Modern NLP relies
heavily on transformer-based architectures, which were introduced in 2017 and now form
the foundation of large language models.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that combines information retrieval
with text generation. Instead of relying only on knowledge stored inside a language
model's parameters, a RAG system first retrieves relevant passages from an external
knowledge source, such as a document collection, and then uses that retrieved content
as context for generating an answer. This approach improves factual accuracy and allows
question answering over private or domain-specific data that the language model was
never trained on.

Applications of AI
AI is used across many industries today, including healthcare for diagnosing diseases,
finance for fraud detection, transportation for self-driving vehicles, and customer
service for chatbots and virtual assistants. As AI systems become more capable, questions
around ethics, bias, transparency, and safety have become increasingly important topics
of research and public discussion.

Challenges in AI
Despite rapid progress, AI systems still face challenges such as lack of common sense
reasoning, difficulty generalizing outside their training data, high computational costs,
and the risk of amplifying biases present in training data. Researchers continue to work
on making AI systems more robust, interpretable, and aligned with human values.
